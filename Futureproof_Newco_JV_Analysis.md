# Futureproof × Newco JV — Review & Recommended Improvements

**Prepared:** 2026-05-16
**Subject:** Outline JV proposal between Futureproof and Newco (Luke Bunbury syndicate) for AU launch of the Equity Preservation Mortgage®

---

## Executive Summary

The outline structure is well-engineered to protect Futureproof's global moat, IP and VC narrative. As drafted it is, however, materially Futureproof-favourable on the economics of the AU book and exposes three risks worth fixing before Newco sees it:

1. The "50/50 JV" headline overstates the actual economic split — Futureproof's 50 bps ARR and 50% end-of-term surplus come off the top globally, leaving Newco with 50% of a smaller residual.
2. The 18-month B2C-only exclusivity does not protect the JV against Futureproof signing a major AU bank as a Product Issuer shortly after launch.
3. 50/50 governance with no deadlock mechanism and annual CEO renewal is operationally fragile and will be read by Newco as a leash.

None of these require concessions on the global red lines (IP, underwriting, pricing, platform, data, brand, compliance, capital-markets, global economics). They can all be fixed by adjusting AU-only economics and the governance machinery.

---

## Top 5 Priority Improvements

Make these five changes before the document goes to Newco. They close ~80% of the gap on Newco's side without moving any of Futureproof's global red lines.

| # | Change | Why it matters |
|---|--------|---------------|
| 1 | **Tier the exclusivity:** 3 years full B2C exclusivity + 2 years most-favoured Product Issuer status, with a **bank-issuer override** (if FP signs a major AU bank as a B2B2C issuer during the window, the JV gets first right to be its retail distribution partner or a defined revenue share). | 18 months is below the threshold where AU broker / adviser distribution compounds. The bank-issuer override removes Newco's biggest fear — that FP licenses CBA / NAB on day 2. |
| 2 | **Give Newco a small AU-only economic share** — either 5–10 bps of the 50 bps ARR on JV-originated mortgages, **or** 10–15% of FP's 50% end-of-term surplus share on the JV book. AU-only, ring-fenced, time- or volume-capped. | Makes the "50/50 JV" headline real. Costs little in early years (no surplus crystallises for 20–30 years) but transforms Newco's modelled IRR. | 3 | **Convert "first right to partner globally" into a defined ROFR** — 30 days to match terms, objective qualification gates (AU performance hurdles met, no compliance breaches), named excluded markets (US always reserved), expires market-by-market, doesn't survive a Newco change of control. **Or drop the global upside entirely** and load more value into option 2. | The current "first right, subject to performance" is the most asymmetric clause in the document. VCs will probe it in DD. Either make it real and bounded, or remove it — a half-promise is worse than either. |

## Full Improvement List

### Economic structure
- Add an AU-only ARR or surplus share for Newco (one of the two, not both — first is cleaner, second is cheaper).
- Cap and time-limit any economic share so the global economics narrative is unambiguous.
- Document explicitly that the global 50 bps ARR and global 50% surplus are untouched.

### Exclusivity and market protection
- Tiered exclusivity: 3 years full B2C + 2 years MFN status.
- Bank-issuer override clause.
- Objective performance hurdles for *maintaining* exclusivity — written as a schedule, no FP discretion.
- Carve-outs always reserved to FP: bank / insurer / super / aged-care / government B2B2C channels.

### Governance
- Deadlock mechanism (escalation → mediation → shotgun clause).
- Independent chair with casting vote on operational matters only.
- Reserved-matters list requiring both shareholders' consent.
- Minimum 3-year CEO term, removal only for cause.
- Related-party transaction protocol with Newco-nominated director approval.
- Pre-emptive rights, drag, tag, transfer restrictions, deadlock-on-exit.

### Global upside
- Defined ROFR replacing "first right" language.
- Objective qualification gates and named excluded markets.
- Time-bound, exhaustible, non-cumulative.
- Tag-along rights on a Futureproof global exit event.

### Risk allocation
- Brand and conduct protection with FP step-in rights for material breach.
- Compliance step-in rights at JV cost.
- Insurance: PI, D&O, cyber carried by JV.
- Mutual indemnities (FP for IP infringement; JV for conduct failures).
- Key-person mechanics on Luke: good/bad leaver, non-compete, non-solicit.

### Drafting and process
- Cut the triple repetition of "red lines" — state once with conviction.
- Lead with what's offered, not what's protected.
- One-page term-sheet appendix with the economic stack in a single table.
- Explicit "what's not in this deal" section (capital markets, B2B, institutional).
- Rename "JV" to "AU Mortgage Originator JV" throughout to make local scope unmistakable.
- Disclose Slipstream upfront.
- Fill the capital budget (Luke's gap currently reads as "you haven't done the work").
- Include the missing deal-structure diagram.
- Remove "begs the question" (used twice, both incorrectly).

### VC DD stress test
- Confirm SaaS framing survives a 30-second pitch with the JV in place.
- Confirm no commitment constrains scaling into 2nd / 3rd / 4th markets.
- Confirm related-party flows (FP→JV licence, FP draw, Slipstream) are clean.
- Confirm Accenture-sourced B2B pipeline is protected if the JV underperforms.

## Supporting Analysis

### Pros (for Futureproof)

**Commercial / strategic**
- Fast path from pre-revenue to post-revenue — the single most important unlock for Pre-A / Series A.
- Skips ~12–18 months of building AU distribution, broker panels and ACL operations.
- Luke Bunbury's network (Challenger, NAB, Volt) is genuinely high-leverage for retirement income distribution and wholesale funder conversations.
- Newco contributes capital, reducing FP's outlay on the B2C arm.
- Preserves the global B2B2C SaaS narrative — structurally the JV is "just another Product Issuer."

**Structural defence of the moat**
- Core 50 bps ARR retained globally, not shared with Newco.
- 50% end-of-term surplus retained globally — ~90% of long-run economics per FP's own data.
- IP, underwriting, pricing, platform, data, brand, capital-markets arranging all sit outside the JV envelope.
- JV licence restricted to B2C retail only, ring-fencing the institutional / capital-markets opportunity.
- Global "first right" is performance-gated and revocable — not an option Newco controls.

**Optionality**
- Doesn't preclude FP from also licensing the EPM to AU banks / insurers via the standard Product Issuer route.
- Creates a controlled in-house lab for new EPM use cases (super top-up, aged care, HNW investor, family assistance) before opening them to bank issuers.

### Cons / risks (for Futureproof)

**Governance and execution**
- 50/50 with no stated deadlock mechanism. "Mutual agreement" appears everywhere (CEO renewal, exit timing, marketing collateral) and creates veto risk in both directions.
- Key-person risk: Luke is named; his exit weakens the distribution thesis materially.
- Annual CEO renewal is operationally fragile — Luke's team won't commit deeply to a role re-voted every year.
- JV is described as "non-core" but will absorb disproportionate founder attention in the first 24 months.

**VC perception**
- "First right to replicate globally" reads as a soft exclusivity to a sharp VC. Even with performance hurdles, every other market is encumbered with a precedent partner who has informational and reputational advantage.
- A 50/50 JV with related-party licensing flows presents as more entangled than a pure SaaS contract.
- Brand risk is asymmetric: brand sits with FP, but compliance / conduct failure happens inside the JV. One Royal-Commission-style article tarnishes EPM globally.

**Commercial holes in the current draft**
- Slipstream Capital carve-out is load-bearing but mentioned only in clause 14.
- No explicit treatment of deadlock, drag, tag, transfer restrictions, future capital calls, or change-of-control.
- 18-month exclusivity is short and internally inconsistent (6 months to launch + 12 months in market).
- No provision for territory conflicts where AU broker partners also operate in NZ / UK.

### Likely view from Newco's side

**"The headline economics are thinner than they look"**
- FP takes 50 bps ARR + 50% end-of-term surplus off the top globally. Newco only ever sees its 25 bps + 50% of *net* JV profits.
- Since FP itself says surplus share is ~90% of long-run value, Newco is being offered 50% of the residual ~10%, plus a distribution margin.
- The "50/50 JV" framing oversells the actual economic split on the AU book.

**"The exclusivity offered is not real protection"**
- 18 months is below the threshold where AU distribution compounds (broker accreditation, lender panel adoption, adviser trust-building run 2–4 years).
- Nothing prevents FP from licensing the EPM to a major AU bank during the window — only to retail B2C competitors. A CBA / NAB B2B2C deployment would crush the JV's retail funnel without breaching the contract.
- B2C-only restriction means the much larger AU institutional opportunity bypasses Newco entirely.

**"The global upside is illusory"**
- "First right to partner, subject to performance" — performance hurdles defined by FP. From Newco's chair this is a unilateral FP option, not a Newco right.
- No tag-along on FP's global exit.

**"Governance is asymmetric"**
- IP veto, brand approval, marketing approval, no sub-licensing, FP retains right to license related entities.
- CEO subject to annual mutual renewal — Luke effectively has tenure at FP's pleasure.
- The "red lines" framing reads as: everything that matters is non-negotiable; everything Newco brings is execution.

**"What about Slipstream?"**
- The capital-markets carve-out will be the first thing a Newco lawyer flags. Newco will want full disclosure of prior commitments before signing.

**Likely Newco counter-asks (in priority order)**
1. Share of FP's 50 bps ARR or end-of-term surplus on the AU book.
2. Exclusivity extended to 3–5 years, OR AU-wide first-look on EPM licensing.
3. Defined, objective performance hurdles for the global "first right."
4. Deadlock / divorce mechanism, independent chair, casting-vote rotation.
5. CEO term protection — minimum 3 years, removal only for cause.
6. Full disclosure of Slipstream and prior commercial commitments.
7. Tag-along rights on a Futureproof global exit event.
8. Pre-emptive participation rights in any new AU Product Issuer FP appoints.

## Closing Position

The structure is sound. The red lines are defensible. The biggest single risk to closing is not the red lines — it's that the **economic split on the AU book is materially less balanced than the "50/50 JV" headline suggests**, and a sophisticated counterparty will model that within an hour.

Decide in advance which of the following Futureproof is willing to give:
- (a) A small AU-only ARR or surplus share to Newco, **or**
- (b) Meaningful extended exclusivity with a bank-issuer override, **or**
- (c) Accept that Newco may walk and execute the original wholly-owned B2C plan.

Better to make that call before the meeting than during it.
