# Warm Intro & Outreach Templates

> **Principles:**
> 1. **Short.** Family office principals delete anything over 200 words on first read.
> 2. **One ask per email.** Multiple asks = decision paralysis = no reply.
> 3. **Make the connector's job easy.** They forward what's frictionless.
> 4. **Lead with the hook, not the company name.** "Retirement Income Covenant" opens more doors than "FutureProof Financial."

---

## 1. The Forwardable Email

Send this to a connector. They forward it (or paste it) to the target. **You write it as if the connector is writing it.**

> **Subject:** Quick intro — FutureProof / Retirement Income Covenant
>
> Hi [Connector],
>
> Could you forward the below to [Target] if you think it's relevant? Happy to take it back if not.
>
> Cheers,
> [Your name]
>
> ---
>
> **Forwardable text below this line — copy/paste anywhere ↓**
>
> ---
>
> Hi [Target first name],
>
> [Connector first name] suggested I reach out.
>
> I'm building **FutureProof** — we turn locked-up home equity into guaranteed retirement income, without forcing retirees to sell or take on a traditional mortgage. The product slots directly into super funds' Retirement Income Covenant obligations, which is the wedge.
>
> We're [ INSERT — current state, e.g. "in regulatory engagement with ASIC and have LOIs from two wholesale funders" ]. Raising via a **Pre-Series A SAFE** (post-money, uncapped, 30% discount to Series A 2028, USD $1M minimum), AU-domiciled (ESVCLP allocation available for qualifying AU investors).
>
> Would 30 minutes be useful? Happy to send a 1-pager first if easier.
>
> [Your name]
> [Mobile] · [Email] · [Calendly]

**Why this works:**
- Connector forwards in 10 seconds (no edits required)
- Target sees connector's name in opening line — credibility transferred
- One sentence on what + one on why now + one on traction + ask
- Calendly link removes scheduling friction
- "Happy to take it back if not" gives connector a graceful out — increases their willingness to forward

## 2. The "Asking for an Intro" Email

Send this to a connector when you want them to make the intro themselves (rather than forward your text).

> **Subject:** Quick favour — intro to [Target name]?
>
> Hi [Connector],
>
> Hope you're well. Quick one — I'm raising for **FutureProof** (the AI-native SaaS platform turning locked-up retiree home equity into guaranteed retirement income). Pre-Series A SAFE, post-money, uncapped, 30% discount to Series A 2028, USD $1M minimum.
>
> [Target] at [Firm] keeps coming up as a strong fit — they back [reason: e.g. "structured financial products / retirement-focused theses / patient capital"]. Would you be comfortable making a quick intro?
>
> If it helps, here's what you could say (feel free to edit):
>
> > [2 sentences about you and what you're building. Make this so good the connector pastes it verbatim.]
>
> Totally fine if it's not the right ask — happy to find another path.
>
> Cheers,
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- "Quick favour" sets a small ask
- Provides ready-to-use copy so the connector doesn't have to think
- "Totally fine if not" preserves the relationship if they decline

## 3. Direct Outreach (when no warm intro exists)

Use sparingly, only for Tier 2 platforms or via a credible event/article hook. **Do not use for Tier 1 single-family offices.**

> **Subject:** Retirement Income Covenant infrastructure — 15 minutes?
>
> Hi [Target first name],
>
> Saw your [recent comment / article / panel] on [topic]. The Retirement Income Covenant is reshaping the alternatives shelf for HNW clients faster than most realised.
>
> I'm building **FutureProof** — we convert locked-up home equity into guaranteed retirement income for the 2.7M Australians sitting on $1M+ homes drawing $30K from super. We slot directly into the gap super funds and advisers are scrambling to fill.
>
> Currently [ INSERT — concrete proof point, e.g. "in regulatory engagement with ASIC, two wholesale funders signed on, Accenture as global collaboration partner" ]. Raising via a **Pre-Series A SAFE** (post-money, uncapped, 30% discount to Series A 2028, USD $1M minimum), AU-domiciled.
>
> 15 minutes next week to share the structure?
>
> [Your name]
> [Mobile] · [Calendly]

**Why this works:**
- Specific reference to something they've said publicly proves you're not spamming
- Lead with a market-level insight (Covenant reshaping shelf), not your company
- Concrete number (2.7M, $1M, $30K) creates tangibility in 1 line
- 15 minutes (not 30) lowers the commitment threshold for cold

## 4. Soft-Sounding Ask (Phase 1)

For pre-launch conversations. **You're not pitching — you're seeking input.** This dramatically lowers the defensive posture.

> **Subject:** Would value your input — 20 minutes?
>
> Hi [Target first name],
>
> [Connector] suggested you'd have sharp views on this.
>
> I'm 4–6 weeks from opening a seed round for **FutureProof** (Retirement Income Covenant infrastructure — home equity into retirement income). Before I do, I want pressure-testing from people who know the AU retirement landscape better than I do.
>
> Specifically: [ INSERT — 1 sentence on the question you actually want their view on, e.g. "how super funds are likely to procure retirement income solutions over the next 24 months" or "how Challenger is likely to react if a third-party EPM gains distribution" ].
>
> Not asking you to invest. Just 20 minutes of your perspective. Coffee on me if you're in [city].
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- "Not asking you to invest" disarms — they can engage without commitment fear
- Specific question signals you've done your homework
- Low friction: 20 minutes, coffee
- People who give you advice often invest later (consistency bias)

## 5. Post-Meeting Follow-Up (within 24 hours)

> **Subject:** Thanks — FutureProof next steps
>
> [Target first name],
>
> Thanks for the time today. Three things, as promised:
>
> 1. **Deck** — attached.
> 2. **[Specific item they asked for]** — [link / attachment / 2-line answer].
> 3. **[Another specific item they asked for]** — [link / attachment / 2-line answer].
>
> A few things worth flagging from the conversation:
>
> - [Concrete commitment they made, e.g. "you'll forward to [name] at Crestone — appreciated"]
> - [What you'll do next, e.g. "I'll come back next Tuesday with the wholesale funder LOI status"]
>
> Anything else I should put in front of you before then?
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- Numbered list of what was promised — proves you listen and execute
- Restates their commitments (locks them in)
- Sets the next touchpoint explicitly
- "Anything else" leaves the door open for them to surface objections privately

## 6. Reference Call Request

When a target asks for references, reply same day with this:

> **Subject:** Reference contacts — FutureProof
>
> [Target first name],
>
> Three references, happy for any/all. Each has agreed to take a call this week:
>
> 1. **[Name]** — [role, firm]. Knows: [what they can speak to, e.g. "regulatory work / model integrity / commercial pipeline"]. Best on: [mobile or email].
> 2. **[Name]** — [role, firm]. Knows: [...]. Best on: [...].
> 3. **[Name]** — [role, firm]. Knows: [...]. Best on: [...].
>
> Let me know who you reach so I can give them a heads-up. Happy to add a fourth if it's useful — who would be most helpful?
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- 3 references with named domains of expertise — you've curated, not dumped
- "Let me know who you reach" gives you intel + courtesy heads-up to references
- Offering a fourth signals confidence

**Reference prep call (do BEFORE you give the contact):**
- Tell each reference who's likely to call
- Tell them what the investor cares about (their published thesis)
- Suggest 2–3 specific points worth raising (without scripting)
- Ask them to text you when the call is done

## 7. The "Nudge After Silence" Email

Use exactly once, 7–10 days after no reply. **Don't send a third email.**

> **Subject:** Re: [original subject]
>
> [Target first name],
>
> Just bumping this up — fully understand if the timing's not right. We've moved a few things along since I wrote: [ INSERT — 1 line of new traction or news, e.g. "added [Name] as advisor" or "first wholesale facility signed" ].
>
> If a pass is the right call, no offence taken — appreciate the directness.
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- Adds new information (gives them a fresh reason to engage)
- Permits a pass — releases the social tension
- Doesn't ask the same question twice; reframes around new traction

---

## 8. The "Pass" Response (turn a no into a future yes)

When you get a pass, reply within 24 hours:

> **Subject:** Re: FutureProof
>
> [Target first name],
>
> Appreciated the directness — and appreciated the time. Two quick asks:
>
> 1. Anyone in your network you think *would* be a fit? Even one name is gold.
> 2. Mind if I keep you on a quarterly update list? No expectation — just useful for next round and you've already got the context.
>
> Either way, thanks again.
>
> [Your name]

**Why this works:**
- Doesn't argue with the pass (futile, damages relationship)
- Asks for one referral — many will give one out of guilt/goodwill
- Quarterly updates put you back on the radar without nagging
- ~30% of family office passes become future yeses if updates are good

---

## Update list (quarterly cadence)

Every "pass" gets added to a quarterly update list. The update should be:

- **3–4 bullets, no more.** Crisp wins, crisp losses, key metric.
- **One concrete number per update** (AUM, customers, revenue, regulatory milestone).
- **No filler, no marketing voice.** Investors see through it.
- **Closing line:** "Happy to share more if useful — and always open to introductions."

The 4th or 5th update is where future yeses come from.
