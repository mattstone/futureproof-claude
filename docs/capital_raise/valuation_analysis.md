### $106M decomposed

The implied $106M market-cleared value is the going-concern value of the company. It can be decomposed as:

> **$106M (going-concern) = ~$20–50M (intrinsic, NAV-equivalent) + goodwill**

Goodwill here means the same thing it does in any acquisition accounting: the difference between book value of identifiable assets and the price an arms-length buyer is willing to pay for the going concern. It is a normal and expected component of any operating company's valuation — not a premium / overvaluation.

The goodwill component reflects:
- **Substantial pre-market development** completed by FP (multi-region calculation engine, AI agent prototype, regulatory engagement, actuarial validation)
- **Partner development** (Accenture, Jones Day, BlackRock, SpiderRock, Atlas SP / Apollo, EY, Dentons — relationships built over years)
- **Senior team confidence signal** — investors paying for execution capability, not just the product
- **GTM proximity** — ~6 months from Australian market launch (Q4/2026)

### Intrinsic vs market-cleared — the conceptual distinction

| Concept | What it measures | FP today |
|---|---|---|
| **Intrinsic value (NAV-equivalent)** | Identifiable assets ex-goodwill: cash, IP book value, tangible assets, contracted revenue | **$20–50M** |
| **Market-cleared value (going-concern with goodwill)** | What arms-length buyers have paid: includes execution capability, partner relationships, brand, GTM proximity | **$106M** |

Both are legitimate measurements — they answer different questions:
- An accountant doing NAV would book $20–50M (depending on how IP is valued: $2M book vs $25–50M going-concern)
- An arms-length investor has been paying $106M and receiving goodwill alongside the identifiable assets
- A strategic acquirer would pay $70–170M for control (going-concern value plus control premium)

### G5. What bends the Y5 answer (sensitivities)

The Y5 exit valuation is most sensitive to three factors:

1. **Series A 2028 clearing price.** Because the SAFE is uncapped, the SAFE investor's effective entry price moves in lockstep with Series A. If Series A clears at $400M pre-money instead of $250M, the SAFE investor's effective entry rises to $280M (vs $175M) — a meaningfully smaller share of the same Y5 exit. This is the principal risk of the uncapped SAFE structure for the investor; the 30% discount + MFN partially compensate. *Investor-conversation note: this is the one place where a thoughtful prospect will probe — be ready to discuss whether a soft cap or valuation floor is on the table.*
2. **Distribution velocity Y1–Y3.** Y5 revenue is built on per-licensee origination assumptions ramping with each new licensee. Y5 revenue would compress to ~$280–350M (vs $463M) if Y2 distribution lags by 12 months — collapsing the Y5 exit range to ~$1.1–2.8B. The first Product Issuer LOI is the single most material milestone for the Y5 trajectory. This is also why the three step-up milestones in Section "Internal value benchmarks" matter — they de-risk the path to the Y5 number.
3. **Multiple compression / strategic premium.** A late-cycle multiple-compression scenario (where SaaS-fintech multiples compress from 6× to 3×) would halve the Y5 exit valuation to ~$1.0–1.4B even with the model holding — implying 2–3× MoM rather than 5–10×. This risk is *not* priced into the SAFE structure (the discount mechanism doesn't protect against multiple compression after Series A converts).
