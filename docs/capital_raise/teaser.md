### Building the Next-Generation of Retirement Income & Funding Products for Every Life Stage.

*Unlocking $25T of locked-up retiree home equity. AI-native SaaS platform. AUS → UK → USA.*

---

**The Problem.**
**2.7M Australians + 36M Americans** are asset-rich and cash-poor — sitting on **$25T of home equity** with no fit-for-purpose product in 35 years. They're told to sell and downsize; most refuse. Australia's Retirement Income Covenant (2022) now legally requires every super fund to have a strategy. Most still don't.

**The Product.**
The **Equity Preservation Mortgage®** — a breakthrough fit-for-purpose retirement mortgage embedded in an AI-native SaaS platform. Converts home equity into tax-free annuity income (1.05–1.50% of home value p.a., per term length). No monthly repayments, no compound interest, no estate erosion. Capital invested in a structured portfolio (~70% equity ETFs / ~30% fixed income) with continuous dynamic hedging via asymmetric collar (cap +40% / floor -20%). Property remains the homeowner's. Profit share at 3-yearly resets (10% to FP); 50/50 split at 30-year maturity.

**Why this works (and reverse mortgages don't).**
- For homeowners: no monthly repayments, no interest accrual eating the estate, lifetime income, equity preserved
- For wholesale funders: **mortgage income with equity-style returns** — long-duration, insured, securitisable, with profit-share upside from S&P appreciation. A new asset class, not a reverse-mortgage variant.
- Run-off mechanism eliminates prepayment risk for funders
- Validated against 50,000-path Monte Carlo (Futureproof's proprietary financial model, 30-year horizon, hard constraints C1–C6 satisfied): **PoC year-30 = 0.03 (3% portfolio capital shortfall probability)**, mean per-mortgage surplus year-30 = **$1.17M (mean) / $4.10M (99%-ile)** (USD)

**Why now.**

- Callaghan Review (2020) explicitly endorsed home equity release as a retirement tool
- AU has reverse-mortgage regulatory precedent under NCCP — clean path
- Demographic wave: 1 in 4 Australians over 65 by 2050; A$3.5T in super needs retirement-phase infrastructure
