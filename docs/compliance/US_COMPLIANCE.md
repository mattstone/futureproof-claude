## 1. REGULATORY CLASSIFICATION

### 1.1 Where Does EPM Sit in US Financial Regulation?

The US has the most fragmented regulatory landscape of all four regions. EPM touches multiple federal and state regimes:

| Category | Regulator | Rules | EPM Fit? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Residential mortgage loan | CFPB | TILA (Reg Z), RESPA (Reg X) | ✅ Primary — secured by dwelling |
| Reverse mortgage (HECM) | HUD/FHA | HECM regulations | ⚠️ Similar but NOT identical — see 1.2 |
| Proprietary reverse mortgage | State regulators | State mortgage lending laws | ⚠️ Possible classification |
| Securities offering | SEC | Securities Act 1933 | ⚠️ If investment component = security |
| Investment advice | SEC / state | Investment Advisers Act 1940 | ⚠️ If advising on portfolio |

### 1.2 EPM vs. HECM (Home Equity Conversion Mortgage)

**HECM is the dominant US reverse mortgage product (FHA-insured):**

| Feature | HECM | EPM | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age requirement | 62+ | Flexible (likely 62+ for US) | Similar |
| FHA insurance | ✅ Required | ❌ Not FHA-insured | **Major difference** |
| HUD counseling | ✅ Mandatory (independent) | ⚠️ Not required (but advisable) | Regulatory gap |
| Loan limit | $1,149,825 (2024) | No FHA limit | EPM can serve higher-value homes |
| No monthly repayments | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Match |
| Consumer receives income | Lump sum, line of credit, or monthly | ✅ Monthly income | Similar |
| Non-recourse | ✅ Yes (FHA guarantee) | ✅ Yes (lender bears risk) | Match |
| Interest compounds | ✅ Yes | ❌ No — paid from investment | **Key difference** |
| Investment portfolio | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | **Unique to EPM** |

### 1.3 EPM as "Proprietary Reverse Mortgage"

**Because EPM is NOT FHA-insured, it's classified as a "proprietary reverse mortgage":**

- Not subject to HECM-specific rules (FHA counseling, loan limits, MIP)
- IS subject to general mortgage lending rules (TILA, RESPA, SAFE Act)
- IS subject to state mortgage lending laws (varies by state)
- May be subject to state reverse mortgage statutes (some states have additional rules)

**This is the simplest regulatory path** — avoid HECM classification entirely, operate as proprietary product.

### 1.4 Securities Classification Risk

**The investment component creates SEC risk:**

| Factor | Analysis |
|---|---|
| Howey Test (investment contract) | Consumer invests money (home equity) → in a common enterprise (pooled fund?) → with expectation of profit (monthly income) → derived from efforts of others (investment manager) |
| Result | ⚠️ **May be a security** under Howey |
| Exemption | Regulation D (private placement) could exempt if limited investors |
| Risk | If classified as security → SEC registration required, massive compliance burden |

**Mitigation Strategies:**

1. **Structure as loan, not investment:** Consumer receives a loan (mortgage); lender independently invests. Consumer receives income from the loan arrangement, not investment returns directly.
2. **No profit expectation from consumer:** Consumer receives fixed or formula-based income, NOT a share of investment returns.
3. **Separation:** Lender manages investment; consumer has no control, choice, or direct ownership of portfolio.

**Recommendation:** Obtain SEC no-action letter or formal opinion before US launch. Structure product to avoid Howey classification.

## 2. NMLS — NATIONWIDE MULTISTATE LICENSING SYSTEM

### 2.1 SAFE Act Requirements

**The Secure and Fair Enforcement for Mortgage Licensing Act (SAFE Act) requires:**

| Entity | Registration | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgage lender (depository institution) | Federal registration | NMLS (via federal regulator — OCC, FDIC, Fed) |
| Mortgage lender (non-depository) | State licence | NMLS (via each state regulator) |
| Mortgage loan originator (MLO) | Individual licence | NMLS (state-by-state) |
| Mortgage servicer | State licence (some states) | NMLS or state regulator |

### 2.2 State-by-State Licensing

**This is the most significant US compliance burden.** Unlike AU/UK/NZ (single national licence), the US requires **separate state licensing:**

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Entity licence | Apply in each state where you operate |
| MLO licence | Each individual originating loans must be licensed per state |
| Net worth requirements | Vary by state ($25,000 - $1,000,000+) |
| Surety bond | Required in most states ($10,000 - $500,000) |
| Background checks | FBI fingerprinting for all MLOs and control persons |
| Pre-licensing education | 20 hours federal + state-specific hours |
| Annual continuing education | 8 hours minimum per MLO |
| Annual renewal | Each state, each year |

### 2.3 Recommended Launch States

**Don't launch in all 50 states. Start with high-value property markets:**

| State | Median Home Value | Regulatory Complexity | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| California | $750,000+ | High (DBO/DFPI licence) | ✅ Phase 1 — largest market |
| New York | $400,000+ | High (DFS licence) | ✅ Phase 1 — high net worth |
| Florida | $400,000+ | Medium (OFR licence) | ✅ Phase 1 — retiree market |
| Texas | $300,000+ | Medium (SML licence) | ⚠️ Phase 2 — homestead protections |
| Washington | $600,000+ | Medium (DFI licence) | ⚠️ Phase 2 |
| Massachusetts | $550,000+ | Medium (DOB licence) | ⚠️ Phase 2 |
| Colorado | $500,000+ | Low-Medium (DORA licence) | ⚠️ Phase 2 |
| Arizona | $400,000+ | Low (DIFI licence) | ✅ Phase 1 — retiree market |
| Hawaii | $800,000+ | Medium (DFI licence) | ⚠️ Phase 2 — high value but small market |

**Phase 1 recommendation: CA, NY, FL, AZ** — covers ~40% of target market.

### 2.4 NMLS Registration Process

| Step | Timeline | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Create NMLS account | 1 day | Company + individual accounts |
| 2. Submit company filing (MU1) | 1-2 weeks | Entity information, control persons, financials |
| 3. Submit MLO filings (MU4) | 1-2 weeks | Individual licence applications |
| 4. FBI fingerprinting | 2-4 weeks | All MLOs + control persons |
| 5. State-specific requirements | 2-8 weeks | Varies — bonds, net worth, state forms |
| 6. State review & approval | 30-90 days | State regulator reviews application |
| 7. Licence issued | After approval | Begin operations in that state |
| **Total** | **3-6 months per state** | Can run states in parallel |

## 3. CFPB — TRID (TILA-RESPA INTEGRATED DISCLOSURE)

### 3.1 Overview

**TRID (also called "Know Before You Owe") requires two key disclosures:**

| Document | When | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Loan Estimate (LE)** | Within 3 business days of application | Standardised cost/terms estimate |
| **Closing Disclosure (CD)** | At least 3 business days before closing | Final costs/terms for consumer review |

### 3.2 Does TRID Apply to EPM?

**TRID applies to most "closed-end consumer credit transactions secured by real property."**

**Exemptions that MAY apply to EPM:**

| Exemption | Reg Z Reference | Does It Apply? |
|---|---|---|
| Reverse mortgage | § 1026.33 | ⚠️ If classified as reverse mortgage, DIFFERENT disclosure (not standard TRID) |
| Open-end credit | § 1026.2(a)(20) | ❌ EPM is closed-end |
| Business purpose | § 1026.3(a) | ❌ EPM is consumer purpose |

**If EPM is classified as reverse mortgage:** Uses TILA reverse mortgage disclosures (§ 1026.33) instead of standard TRID. These include:
- Total Annual Loan Cost (TALC) disclosure
- Projected loan balance table
- Right of rescission (3 business days)

**If EPM is NOT classified as reverse mortgage:** Standard TRID applies (Loan Estimate + Closing Disclosure).

**Recommendation:** Classify as proprietary reverse mortgage — uses § 1026.33 disclosures, which are better suited to EPM's structure (no monthly payments, accruing balance).

### 3.3 Loan Estimate — Key Fields for EPM

**Even under reverse mortgage disclosure, these elements must be provided:**

| Field | EPM Content |
|---|---|
| Loan amount | Home value × LVR (e.g., $640,000 on $800,000 home) |
| Interest rate | Lender margin rate (e.g., 5.5% p.a.) |
| Monthly payment | **$0** (consumer makes no payments) |
| Monthly income | Expected disbursement (pessimistic/expected/optimistic) |
| Estimated total costs | Origination fees, annual fees, closing costs |
| Cash to close | **$0** (or refund if paying off existing mortgage) |
| APR | Annualised cost including all fees |
| Total interest cost | Estimated over loan term |
| Projected loan balance | Year-by-year projection (multiple scenarios) |

### 3.4 Total Annual Loan Cost (TALC) — Reverse Mortgage Specific

**§ 1026.33 requires TALC disclosure showing:**

| Scenario | Projection Period | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Low appreciation (0%) | Life expectancy | Projected balance, total cost, TALC rate |
| Moderate appreciation (4%) | Life expectancy | Projected balance, total cost, TALC rate |
| High appreciation (8%) | Life expectancy | Projected balance, total cost, TALC rate |

**TALC must show the cost AS IF the consumer were paying** — this reveals the true cost of the mortgage even though consumer pays nothing directly.

### 3.5 Right of Rescission

**Under TILA § 1026.23:**
- Consumer has 3 business days after closing to rescind (cancel) the transaction
- Applies to refinances and home equity transactions (NOT purchase money)
- EPM triggers rescission right ✅
- Lender must provide 2 copies of rescission notice
- No funds disbursed until rescission period expires

### 3.6 Timing Requirements

| Disclosure | Deadline | Penalty for Late |
|---|---|---|
| Loan Estimate | 3 business days after application | TILA violation — civil liability |
| Closing Disclosure | 3 business days before closing | Must delay closing |
| Rescission notice | At closing | Right extends to 3 years if not provided |
| Changed circumstances (revised LE) | 3 business days of change | Must re-disclose |
| Annual statements | Within 30 days of anniversary | TILA violation |

- [ ] Loan Estimate generator (auto-populated from application data)
- [ ] Closing Disclosure generator
- [ ] TALC calculator (3 appreciation scenarios)
- [ ] Rescission notice template (2 copies)
- [ ] 3-day LE delivery tracking (application date → LE sent date)

## 4. APPRAISAL STANDARDS

### 4.1 Federal Appraisal Requirements

**Under FIRREA (Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery, and Enforcement Act):**

| Requirement | Detail | EPM Application |
|---|---|---|
| Licensed/certified appraiser | State-licensed or certified | ✅ Mandatory for all EPM |
| USPAP compliance | Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice | ✅ Required |
| Independence | Appraiser must be independent of lender | ✅ No in-house appraisals |
| De minimis threshold | Below $400,000 → evaluation (not full appraisal) | ⚠️ EPM homes likely exceed threshold |
| Appraisal Management Company (AMC) | If using AMC, must be state-registered | ✅ Recommended approach |

### 4.2 Appraisal Requirements for EPM

| Factor | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Type | Full interior/exterior appraisal (URAR — Uniform Residential Appraisal Report) |
| Form | Fannie Mae Form 1004 / Freddie Mac Form 70 |
| Comparable sales | Minimum 3 comparable sales within 12 months, 1 mile |
| Property condition | Must assess condition and remaining economic life |
| As-is value | Current market value (not future projected) |
| Environmental hazards | Must note if observed (lead paint, asbestos, flooding) |
| Delivery | Copy to consumer within 3 business days of completion (ECOA) |

### 4.3 Appraisal Independence Requirements (AIR)

**Dodd-Frank § 1472 (Reg Z § 1026.42):**

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| No coercion | Cannot pressure appraiser to meet target value |
| No conflicts | Loan officers cannot select or influence appraiser |
| Separation | Appraisal ordering separated from loan production |
| Compensation | Cannot vary appraiser pay based on outcome |
| Reporting | Appraiser must report coercion attempts to state board |

### 4.4 Automated Valuation Models (AVMs)

**For ongoing monitoring (not origination):**

| AVM Provider | Coverage | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| Zillow Zestimate | Nationwide | Monitoring / marketing |
| CoreLogic | Nationwide | Monitoring / trigger events |
| HouseCanary | Nationwide | Analytics |
| FHFA AVM standards | Regulatory | Must meet accuracy thresholds |

**New rule (2024):** Federal banking agencies finalized AVM quality control standards. AVMs must be validated for accuracy, monitored for bias, and compliant with fair lending laws.

### 4.5 Platform Implementation

- [ ] AMC relationship established (or direct appraiser panel)
- [ ] Appraisal ordering workflow (separated from loan production)
- [ ] URAR (Form 1004) intake and storage
- [ ] Consumer appraisal copy delivery (3 business days — ECOA)
- [ ] AVM integration for ongoing monitoring (Zillow API or CoreLogic)
- [ ] Annual revaluation trigger system
- [ ] AIR compliance documented (no coercion policy)

## 5. FAIR LENDING & CONSUMER PROTECTION

### 5.1 Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA / Reg B)

**Cannot discriminate in credit decisions based on:**
- Race, color, religion, national origin, sex
- Marital status, age (provided applicant can contract)
- Receipt of public assistance income
- Good faith exercise of rights under Consumer Credit Protection Act

**EPM-Specific Risks:**

| Risk | Scenario | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Age discrimination | Only offering to 62+ | ✅ Permitted — bona fide qualification (aligned with reverse mortgage) |
| Property location (redlining) | Declining properties in certain neighborhoods | ❌ Prohibited — must use objective criteria |
| Income source | Declining consumers on public assistance | ❌ Prohibited — cannot reject based on income source |
| Marital status | Requiring both spouses to apply | ⚠️ Cannot require — but should encourage (estate protection) |

### 5.2 Fair Housing Act (FHA)

**Additional protections in housing-related credit:**
- Cannot discriminate based on familial status or disability
- Must make reasonable accommodations for disabled applicants
- Marketing must not target/exclude protected classes

### 5.3 Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA / Reg C)

**If originating >25 closed-end mortgage loans per year:**
- Must report loan-level data to CFPB
- Data includes: applicant demographics, loan terms, property location, action taken
- Data publicly available (transparency)
- Used by regulators to detect fair lending violations

**EPM will likely trigger HMDA reporting** once operational at scale.

### 5.4 Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)

**Dodd-Frank § 1031 — CFPB's primary enforcement tool:**

| Standard | Definition | EPM Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Unfair | Causes substantial injury, not reasonably avoidable, not outweighed by benefits | Unclear fee structures, hidden costs |
| Deceptive | Misleading representation or omission likely to mislead | Overstating expected income, hiding risks |
| Abusive | Takes unreasonable advantage of consumer's lack of understanding, inability to protect interests, or reliance on firm | Targeting vulnerable elderly, complex product |

**CFPB has been aggressive on UDAAP enforcement.** EPM's complexity and elderly target demographic = high scrutiny risk.

**Mitigation:**
- [ ] Plain language disclosures (8th grade reading level)
- [ ] All income projections include pessimistic scenario prominently
- [ ] "This is not a guarantee" language on all projections
- [ ] Fee schedule clear and upfront (no hidden fees)
- [ ] Independent counseling recommended (even if not HUD-mandated)
- [ ] Cooling-off period before closing

### 5.5 Platform Implementation

- [ ] ECOA adverse action notice template
- [ ] Fair lending policy documented
- [ ] HMDA data collection integrated into application
- [ ] HMDA LAR (Loan Application Register) maintained
- [ ] UDAAP compliance review of all consumer-facing content
- [ ] Plain language review (readability score)
- [ ] Marketing compliance review (no targeting/excluding protected classes)

## 6. HUD COUNSELING (VOLUNTARY BUT RECOMMENDED)

### 6.1 HECM vs. EPM Counseling

**HECM requires HUD-approved counseling before application. EPM does not.**

However, voluntarily offering counseling:
- Reduces UDAAP risk (consumer was informed by independent party)
- Reduces complaint/litigation risk
- Demonstrates good faith to CFPB
- May be required by some state reverse mortgage statutes

### 6.2 Recommended Counseling Framework

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Provider | HUD-approved housing counseling agencies |
| Timing | Before application (encourage, don't require) |
| Cost | Free to consumer (FutureProof or HUD funds) |
| Content | EPM explanation, alternatives, consumer rights, estate impact |
| Certificate | Counseling completion certificate stored in application file |
| Disclosure | "We recommend you speak with a HUD-approved counselor before applying" |

### 6.3 Platform Implementation

- [ ] HUD counseling agency locator link on application page
- [ ] Counseling completion certificate upload field
- [ ] "Recommended but not required" disclosure
- [ ] Track counseling completion rate (for compliance monitoring)
