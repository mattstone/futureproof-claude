## 1. REGULATORY CLASSIFICATION

### 1.1 Is EPM a "Consumer Credit Contract" Under NZ Law?

**Yes.** Under the CCCFA 2003, a "consumer credit contract" is a contract where:
1. A creditor provides credit to a debtor (✅ — loan secured on property)
2. The debtor is a natural person (✅)
3. The credit is for personal, domestic, or household purposes (✅)

**EPM Classification Under NZ Law:**

| Category | Legislation | Fit? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer credit contract | CCCFA 2003 | ✅ Primary classification | Secured credit, consumer purpose |
| Mortgage | Property Law Act 2007 | ✅ Yes | Security interest in land |
| Financial advice | Financial Markets Conduct Act 2013 | ⚠️ Possibly | If investment component = financial advice |
| Managed investment scheme | FMC Act, Part 4 | ⚠️ Possibly | If pooled investment fund |

**NZ does NOT have a specific "equity release" or "lifetime mortgage" category** (unlike UK). EPM falls under general consumer credit + mortgage law.

### 1.2 Required Registrations & Licences

| Registration | Body | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Financial Service Provider (FSP) | Companies Office (FSPR) | ✅ Mandatory | Must register before providing financial services |
| Dispute Resolution Scheme | FSCL, FDRS, or IFSO | ✅ Mandatory | Must join approved scheme |
| Licensed Financial Advice Provider (FAP) | FMA | ⚠️ Likely | If providing financial advice on investment component |
| Creditor registration | CCCFA | ✅ Required | Must comply with creditor obligations |
| Fit & Proper Person | FMA | ✅ Required for directors/officers | Directors assessed |

**Key Difference from AU/UK:** NZ does not require a specific lending licence (no equivalent of ACL or FCA authorisation). Registration as FSP + compliance with CCCFA is the pathway.

## 2. CCCFA — RESPONSIBLE LENDING

### 2.1 Lender Responsibility Principles (Part 1A CCCFA)

**The CCCFA 2021 amendments introduced strict responsible lending obligations:**

**Principle 1: Make reasonable inquiries (s9C(3)(a))**

| Inquiry | Purpose | EPM Application |
|---|---|---|
| Income & expenses | Assess ability to meet obligations | ⚠️ Consumer has minimal obligations (property maintenance only) |
| Assets & liabilities | Net position | ✅ Home value, existing mortgages |
| Likely changes | Future income changes (retirement) | ✅ Retirement income planning |
| Requirements & objectives | Why do they want credit? | ✅ Supplementary income |
| Existing credit commitments | Over-commitment check | ✅ Any existing mortgage |

**Principle 2: Verify information (s9C(3)(b))**
- Verify income using bank statements, IRD records, or employer confirmation
- Verify property value using registered valuer or QV (Quotable Value)
- Verify existing debts using credit bureau (Centrix, Equifax NZ, illion NZ)

**Principle 3: Assess suitability (s9C(3)(c))**

The lender must be satisfied the credit is **"not unsuitable"**, considering:
- Consumer can meet obligations without substantial hardship
- Credit meets consumer's requirements and objectives
- For EPM: Consumer's ongoing obligations are property maintenance, rates, insurance

### 2.2 EPM-Specific Responsible Lending Considerations

**The 2021 CCCFA amendments were controversial** (overly prescriptive expense verification). Some were walked back in 2023-2024, but core principles remain:

| Factor | Pre-2021 | Post-2021 | EPM Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Expense verification | General inquiry | Detailed verification | ⚠️ Must verify consumer can cover rates, insurance, maintenance |
| Income verification | Reasonable steps | Bank statements required | ✅ Verify existing income (pension, super) |
| Future changes | Consider if known | Proactively assess | ✅ Retirement timeline, health |
| Alternative options | Not required | Consider alternatives | ✅ Document why EPM vs. alternatives |

**Consumer's Ongoing Obligations Under EPM:**

| Obligation | Typical Annual Cost | Verification |
|---|---|---|
| Council rates | $2,500 - $6,000 | Rates assessment from council |
| Buildings insurance | $1,500 - $4,000 | Current insurance policy |
| Property maintenance | $2,000 - $5,000 | Estimate based on property condition |
| Body corporate (if unit) | $3,000 - $8,000 | Body corporate levy schedule |
| **Total** | **$6,000 - $23,000** | Consumer must demonstrate capacity |

**Assessment:** Even though consumer makes no loan repayments, they must demonstrate they can cover these property-related costs from existing income + EPM income.

### 2.3 Disclosure Requirements

**CCCFA requires comprehensive pre-contractual disclosure:**

| Disclosure | CCCFA Reference | EPM Content |
|---|---|---|
| Initial disclosure statement | s17 | Total loan amount, interest rate, fees |
| Annual cost rate | s21 | Effective annual cost including all fees |
| Total interest charges | s22 | ⚠️ Complex — interest paid from investment, not consumer |
| Fees | s23 | All fees itemised (establishment, annual, exit) |
| Security interest | s24 | Mortgage over residential property |
| Default consequences | s25 | ⚠️ Modified — lender bears risk, but property could be sold |
| Hardship rights | s27 | Consumer's right to apply for hardship variation |
| Dispute resolution | s27 | Named scheme (FSCL, FDRS, or IFSO) |

**Continuing Disclosure:**
- Annual statement showing loan balance, interest charged, income paid (s30-33)
- Notify consumer of any change to terms (s22-23)
- Provide updated disclosure if interest rate changes

## 3. LVR — RESERVE BANK RESTRICTIONS

### 3.1 RBNZ LVR Framework

**The RBNZ imposes LVR restrictions on banks (not non-bank lenders directly):**

| Category | Current LVR Limit | Speed Limit (% above limit) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Owner-occupied (new lending) | 80% | 15% of new lending can exceed | Standard residential |
| Investment property | 65% | 5% of new lending can exceed | Stricter for investors |
| First home buyers | 80% | 15% | Same as owner-occupied |

### 3.2 Does RBNZ LVR Apply to EPM?

**If the lender is a registered bank:** ✅ Yes — RBNZ LVR restrictions apply directly.

**If the lender is a non-bank lender:** ❌ No — RBNZ LVR restrictions don't apply directly, but:
- Wholesale funders (banks) may impose LVR limits on non-bank lenders they fund
- Market expectations align with RBNZ limits
- Prudent practice to follow RBNZ guidelines regardless

### 3.3 EPM LVR Framework (NZ)

**Recommended Maximum LVR: 80%** (consistent with RBNZ and AU approach)

**Property Valuation:**

| Method | Provider | Acceptable? |
|---|---|---|
| Registered Valuer | NZIV-registered | ✅ Gold standard |
| QV (Quotable Value) | CoreLogic NZ (owns QV) | ✅ Desktop/automated |
| Bank e-valuation | Lender's own system | ⚠️ Only for low LVR (<60%) |

**Valuation Frequency:**
- At origination: Full registered valuer report
- Annual: QV desktop or e-valuation (acceptable for monitoring)
- Trigger events: Full revaluation (market correction, damage, consumer request)

## 4. KIWISAVER INTERACTION

### 4.1 How EPM Affects KiwiSaver

**KiwiSaver is NZ's workplace savings scheme. Key interactions:**

| Factor | Impact | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| First Home Withdrawal | ⚠️ Blocks future use | Consumer has already used property; cannot use KiwiSaver for another first home |
| KiwiSaver contributions | ❌ No impact | EPM income is not "employment income" — no employer contributions |
| KiwiSaver withdrawal (retirement, 65+) | ❌ No impact | Consumer can still withdraw KiwiSaver independently |
| Hardship withdrawal | ⚠️ Possible alternative | Consumer might withdraw KiwiSaver instead of taking EPM |

### 4.2 KiwiSaver First Home Grant

**Kāinga Ora First Home Grant:** Not relevant — EPM targets existing homeowners.

However, if consumer previously used KiwiSaver First Home Withdrawal to purchase their property:
- No restriction on subsequently taking an EPM
- No clawback of KiwiSaver withdrawal

### 4.3 Disclosure Required

> "Taking an EPM does not affect your KiwiSaver balance or contributions. However, if you are considering using KiwiSaver funds as an alternative to EPM, we recommend you speak with a financial adviser to compare options."

**Key Message:** EPM and KiwiSaver are independent. No conflict, but consumer should consider whether KiwiSaver drawdown is a better option (general advice only).

## 5. MĀORI LAND RESTRICTIONS

### 5.1 Te Ture Whenua Maori Act 1993 (Māori Land Act)

**Māori freehold land has special restrictions on alienation (sale, mortgage, lease):**

| Restriction | Detail | EPM Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Cannot be mortgaged without court approval | Māori Land Court must consent | ✅ Significant barrier |
| Preferred classes of alienees | Whanau, hapū, iwi have right of first refusal | ⚠️ On sale, lender may not recover easily |
| Occupation orders | Court can grant occupation to whānau | ⚠️ May conflict with lender's security |
| Succession | Land may pass through Māori succession rules | ⚠️ Complex estate planning |

### 5.2 EPM on Māori Land — Feasibility

**Highly restricted.** In practice:

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Court approval | Required for mortgage — may take months, uncertain outcome |
| Valuation | Limited comparable sales data (land rarely sold) |
| Recovery on default | Lender cannot easily sell — preferred alienees have rights |
| Market | Very small — most Māori freehold land is rural/communal |

**Recommendation:** **Exclude Māori freehold land from EPM at launch.** 

This is NOT discrimination — it's a practical limitation of the security interest:
- The land cannot be freely mortgaged (legal restriction)
- The lender cannot enforce security in the normal way
- The valuation methodology doesn't work for this land type

**If requested by iwi/hapū:** Engage specialist Māori land lawyer and consider bespoke product structure. This would be a separate Phase 2 product development.

**Disclosure:**
> "EPM is currently available for general land (freehold and cross-lease). Māori freehold land under Te Ture Whenua Maori Act 1993 has specific restrictions on mortgaging that make the standard EPM product unsuitable. If you have questions about Māori land, please contact us for a specialist referral."

## 6. NZ SUPERANNUATION (PENSION) INTERACTION

### 6.1 NZ Super Income Test

**NZ Superannuation is largely universal (not income-tested for eligibility):**

| Factor | Detail | EPM Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Eligibility | Age 65+, NZ resident 10+ years | ❌ No impact — EPM doesn't affect eligibility |
| Income test | **No income test** for NZ Super | ✅ Major advantage — EPM income doesn't reduce pension |
| Abatement | Applies only to those still working (wages) | ❌ No impact on EPM |
| Tax on NZ Super | Taxed as income (PAYE) | ✅ NZ Super taxed, but EPM income treatment separate |

**This is a significant NZ advantage over Australia** — EPM income does NOT reduce NZ Super.

### 6.2 Residential Care Subsidy

**If consumer enters aged care:**

| Factor | Detail | EPM Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Asset test | Assets below threshold → subsidy | ⚠️ Investment portfolio is an asset; home may be exempt |
| Home exemption | Principal residence exempt if spouse still living there | ✅ Spouse protection |
| Portfolio value | Counted as assessable asset | ⚠️ May reduce care subsidy |

**Disclosure:**
> "If you enter residential care, the investment portfolio associated with your EPM may be counted as an assessable asset for the Residential Care Subsidy. This could affect your eligibility. We recommend discussing aged care planning with a financial adviser."

## 7. AML/CFT — ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING

### 7.1 AML/CFT Act 2009

**FutureProof (or NZ lender) is a "reporting entity" under the AML/CFT Act:**

| Obligation | Detail | EPM Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| Risk assessment | Business-wide ML/TF risk assessment | Documented, board-approved |
| AML/CFT programme | Written compliance programme | DIA-supervised (or FMA if licensed) |
| CDD | Customer due diligence | Identity verification, address, source of funds |
| Enhanced CDD | PEPs, high-risk, complex structures | Additional verification |
| Ongoing CDD | Transaction monitoring | Monitor income disbursements |
| Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR) | Report to FIU (NZ Police) | Via goAML system |
| Prescribed Transaction Reports (PTR) | Cash >$10,000, international wire >$1,000 | Via goAML system |
| Record retention | 5 years after relationship | All CDD records |

### 7.2 Identity Verification Standard

**NZ Electronic Identity Verification (EIV):**

| Document | Acceptable? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NZ Passport | ✅ Primary | Photo ID |
| NZ Driver Licence | ✅ Primary | Photo ID |
| NZ Firearms Licence | ✅ Primary | Photo ID |
| Birth certificate | ⚠️ Secondary only | No photo |
| Bank statement | ⚠️ Address verification | Not ID |
| RealMe verified identity | ✅ Digital | Government digital identity |

**RealMe integration recommended** — government-backed digital identity, high trust, reduces friction.

### 7.3 Supervisor

| Entity Type | AML/CFT Supervisor |
|---|---|
| Registered bank | RBNZ |
| Non-bank lender (NBDT) | FMA |
| Financial adviser | FMA |
| Other reporting entity | DIA (Department of Internal Affairs) |

## 8. PRIVACY — INFORMATION PRIVACY PRINCIPLES (IPPs)

### 8.1 Privacy Act 2020

**NZ Privacy Act 2020 replaced the 1993 Act. 13 Information Privacy Principles (IPPs):**

| IPP | Requirement | EPM Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| IPP 1 | Purpose of collection | Collect only for EPM assessment and servicing |
| IPP 2 | Source of information | Collect from consumer directly (where practicable) |
| IPP 3 | Collection from subject | Tell consumer what you're collecting and why |
| IPP 4 | Manner of collection | Lawful, fair, not unreasonably intrusive |
| IPP 5 | Storage & security | Reasonable security safeguards |
| IPP 6 | Access to personal information | Consumer can request access |
| IPP 7 | Correction | Consumer can request correction |
| IPP 8 | Accuracy | Check accuracy before using |
| IPP 9 | Retention | Don't keep longer than needed (but 7yr regulatory minimum) |
| IPP 10 | Use limitation | Only for purpose collected |
| IPP 11 | Disclosure limitation | Only disclose with consent or legal authority |
| IPP 12 | Cross-border disclosure | Must ensure comparable protection |
| IPP 13 | Unique identifiers | Don't assign government IDs as identifiers |

### 8.2 Mandatory Breach Notification

**Privacy Act 2020, Part 6A (Notifiable Privacy Breaches):**

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger | Breach "likely to cause serious harm" |
| Notify | Privacy Commissioner + affected individuals |
| Timeline | "As soon as practicable" after becoming aware |
| Penalty (failure) | Up to $10,000 (individual), $50,000 (body corporate) — but proposed increase |
| Commissioner powers | Investigation, compliance notice, access direction |

### 8.3 Credit Reporting

**Credit Reporting Privacy Code 2004 (updated):**
- EPM reported to credit bureaus (Centrix, Equifax NZ, illion NZ)
- Positive and negative data reported
- Consumer can request free credit report annually
- Default listing: Only after 30+ days and $100+ overdue

**EPM-Specific:** Consumer makes no repayments, so no payment history. Account opening, limit, and any default/hardship are reported.

## 9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 9.1 Mandatory Membership

**All FSPs must belong to an approved dispute resolution scheme:**

| Scheme | Full Name | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| FSCL | Financial Services Complaints Limited | General financial services |
| FDRS | Financial Dispute Resolution Service | Banking, lending |
| IFSO | Insurance & Financial Services Ombudsman | Insurance, financial advice |

**Recommended for EPM:** FDRS (most relevant for lending/mortgage products)

### 9.2 Process

| Step | Timeline | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Internal complaint | Consumer complains to FutureProof | Must be resolved or deadlocked |
| Internal resolution | Within 40 working days | Best efforts to resolve |
| Deadlock | At 40 days | Issue deadlock letter, refer to scheme |
| Scheme investigation | Varies (typically 2-6 months) | Independent investigation |
| Decision | Binding on firm | Up to $350,000 compensation |

## 10. TAX TREATMENT (NZ)

### 10.1 Income Tax

| Income Type | Tax Treatment | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| EPM monthly income | ⚠️ Likely assessable income | IRD may treat as income from investment |
| NZ Super | Taxed (PAYE) | Separate from EPM |
| KiwiSaver withdrawal | Tax-free (contributions already taxed) | Separate from EPM |
| Investment returns (portfolio) | Taxable under FIF rules | Foreign Investment Fund regime (if ETF is offshore) |

### 10.2 Foreign Investment Fund (FIF) Rules

**If the investment portfolio includes offshore ETFs (e.g., S&P 500):**

| Rule | Detail | EPM Impact |
|---|---|---|
| FIF regime | NZ residents taxed on offshore portfolio income | ✅ Applies to EPM investment portfolio |
| FDR method | Fair Dividend Rate — 5% of opening market value deemed income | Most common method |
| Threshold | Applies if total FIF interests >$50,000 | ✅ EPM portfolio will exceed this |
| Tax rate | Individual's marginal rate (10.5% - 39%) | Applied to 5% deemed income |

**Example:** Portfolio value $640,000 × 5% FDR = $32,000 deemed income → taxed at marginal rate.

**Critical Question:** Who pays FIF tax — the consumer or the lender?
- If consumer owns the portfolio → Consumer pays FIF tax on deemed income
- If lender/trust structure owns portfolio → Different tax treatment
- **Must be clarified in product design — significant impact on net income to consumer**
