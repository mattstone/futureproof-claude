## 1. REGULATORY CLASSIFICATION

### 1.1 Where Does EPM Sit in UK Financial Regulation?

The UK has specific product categories that determine regulatory treatment:

| Product Type | Regulator | Rules | EPM Fit? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regulated mortgage contract | FCA | MCOB | ⚠️ Partially — secured on land, but no repayments |
| Equity release (lifetime mortgage) | FCA | MCOB Chapter 12 | ✅ **Closest fit** — loan secured on home, no regular repayments |
| Home reversion plan | FCA | MCOB Chapter 12 | ❌ No — EPM doesn't transfer ownership |
| Consumer credit | FCA | CONC | ❌ No — secured on land (excluded from CONC) |
| Managed investment | FCA | COBS | ⚠️ Possibly — investment portfolio component |

### 1.2 EPM as "Equity Release" — Lifetime Mortgage Classification

**Under MCOB 12, a "lifetime mortgage" is a regulated mortgage contract where:**
1. Entry into the contract is restricted to older borrowers (typically 55+)
2. Repayment of capital is NOT required until a specified event (death, move to care, sale)
3. Interest may roll up (compound) rather than be paid monthly

**EPM Comparison:**

| Feature | Lifetime Mortgage | EPM | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age restriction | 55+ (typically) | Flexible (but likely 55+) | ✅ Similar |
| No monthly repayments | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Match |
| Interest rolls up | ✅ Typically | ❌ No — paid from investment returns | ⚠️ Differs |
| Consumer receives income | ❌ Usually lump sum or drawdown | ✅ Monthly income | ⚠️ Differs |
| Negative equity guarantee | ✅ Required (ERC) | ✅ Consumer bears no risk | ✅ Match |
| Lender bears risk | ⚠️ Partially | ✅ Fully | ✅ Stronger |

**Regulatory Position:** EPM is most likely classified as a **lifetime mortgage** under MCOB 12, with additional features (investment component, income stream). FCA may require a product-specific variation or waiver.

**Recommendation:** Engage FCA Innovation Hub (regulatory sandbox) before launch. EPM's consumer-risk-free structure may qualify for expedited review.

### 1.3 Required Authorisations

| Authorisation | Purpose | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| FCA Authorisation (FSMA Part 4A) | Carry on regulated activity | ✅ Mandatory |
| Regulated mortgage activity | Advising, arranging, entering into mortgages | ✅ Mandatory |
| Home finance mediation | Broker/intermediary activity | ✅ If using brokers |
| Investment business | Managing/advising on investments | ⚠️ Likely — portfolio component |
| Permissions: "entering into a regulated mortgage contract" | Lender permission | ✅ Mandatory |
| Permissions: "advising on regulated mortgage contracts" | Advisory permission | ✅ If providing advice |

**FCA Register:** All authorised firms listed at https://register.fca.org.uk/

## 2. MCOB — MORTGAGE CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

### 2.1 MCOB Overview

MCOB is the FCA's sourcebook for mortgage conduct. Key chapters:

| Chapter | Title | EPM Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| MCOB 1 | Application and purpose | Scope determination |
| MCOB 2 | Conduct standards | General conduct requirements |
| MCOB 3A | Financial promotions | Marketing and advertising rules |
| MCOB 4 | Advising and selling | Pre-sale obligations |
| MCOB 5 | Pre-application disclosure | KFI (Key Features Illustration) |
| MCOB 5A | Disclosure at start of contract | Contract disclosure |
| MCOB 6 | Disclosure at start of contract & after sale | Post-sale disclosure |
| MCOB 7 | Arrears, payment shortfalls, repossessions | ⚠️ Modified for EPM |
| MCOB 9 | Equity release (lifetime mortgages) | ✅ **Primary chapter** |
| MCOB 12 | Equity release detailed rules | ✅ **Primary chapter** |
| MCOB 13 | Arrears and repossessions (equity release) | ✅ Applies |

### 2.2 MCOB 12 — Equity Release Specific Rules

**Key requirements for lifetime mortgages (EPM must comply):**

**2.2.1 Advice Requirement**
- MCOB 12.3: Lifetime mortgages can **only be sold on an advised basis** (no execution-only)
- The adviser must hold specific equity release qualifications (CeRER — Certificate in Equity Release)
- Adviser must be member of the Equity Release Council (ERC) or equivalent

**Impact on EPM:** Cannot offer self-service online-only applications. Every consumer must receive regulated advice from a qualified adviser before entering EPM contract.

**2.2.2 Personalised Illustration**
- MCOB 12.4: Must provide a personalised illustration before application
- Must follow ERC illustration standards
- Must show:
  - Total amount of loan
  - Interest rate and how it's charged
  - Projected loan balance at death/sale (multiple scenarios)
  - Impact on estate value
  - Consumer's right to remain in property
  - "No negative equity guarantee" statement

**EPM Personalised Illustration Must Also Show:**
- Expected monthly income (pessimistic, expected, optimistic)
- Investment portfolio structure (ETF/annuity split)
- Lender margin deduction
- Scenarios: What happens if investment returns are low/zero/negative
- Statement: "You will never owe more than your home is worth"
- Statement: "The lender bears all investment risk"

**2.2.3 No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG)**
- MCOB 12.5: All lifetime mortgages MUST include NNEG
- Consumer (or estate) will never owe more than property value
- EPM already satisfies this (consumer bears zero risk)
- NNEG must be stated prominently in contract and illustration

**2.2.4 Right to Remain**
- Consumer has right to remain in property for life
- Lender cannot require sale while consumer occupies as principal residence
- Consumer must maintain property in reasonable condition
- Consumer must continue paying council tax, insurance, maintenance

### 2.3 MCOB 3A — Financial Promotions

**All EPM marketing must comply with FCA financial promotions rules:**

| Rule | Requirement | EPM Application |
|---|---|---|
| Fair, clear, not misleading | All promotions must be balanced | ✅ Cannot overstate income projections |
| Risk warnings | Must include appropriate risk warnings | ✅ "Your home is at risk" warning |
| Past performance | Cannot guarantee future returns | ✅ Monte Carlo projections are scenarios, not guarantees |
| Comparisons | Must be fair and use like-for-like | ✅ Cannot compare EPM to savings accounts |
| Target audience | Must be appropriate for intended audience | ✅ Retired/semi-retired homeowners |

**Mandatory Warning (Equity Release):**
> "This is a lifetime mortgage. To understand the features and risks, ask for a personalised illustration."

**Additional EPM Warning:**
> "Your home is used as security. The value of investments can go down as well as up. You should seek independent financial advice."

### 2.4 MCOB 4 — Advising and Selling Standards

**Advised sales process (mandatory for equity release):**

1. **Initial Disclosure Document (IDD)** — Who the adviser is, what they offer, how they're paid
2. **Fact Find** — Comprehensive assessment of consumer's circumstances:
   - Income, expenditure, assets, liabilities
   - Health and lifestyle (affects life expectancy projections)
   - Property details and valuation
   - Existing mortgage(s)
   - Beneficiaries and estate planning wishes
   - Alternative options considered
3. **Suitability Report** — Written explanation of why EPM is suitable:
   - Consumer's needs and objectives
   - Why EPM (vs. alternatives like downsizing, standard equity release, pension drawdown)
   - Risks and mitigations
   - Cost analysis
4. **Personalised Illustration** — Detailed projection (see 2.2.2 above)
5. **Cooling-off Period** — Consumer must have time to consider (no high-pressure tactics)

## 3. AFFORDABILITY ASSESSMENT

### 3.1 FCA Affordability Rules for Equity Release

**Traditional affordability (can consumer repay?) does NOT apply** — consumer makes no repayments. However, FCA still requires assessment of:

| Factor | Assessment | EPM Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Property maintenance | Can consumer maintain the property? | Council tax, buildings insurance, repairs |
| Ongoing obligations | Can consumer meet living costs? | EPM income should supplement, not replace |
| Interest compounding | Will debt exceed property value? | NNEG applies — lender bears risk |
| Alternative options | Has consumer considered alternatives? | Downsizing, pension drawdown, standard ER |
| Vulnerability | Is consumer vulnerable? | Age, health, cognitive capacity, isolation |

### 3.2 Consumer Duty (July 2023)

**The FCA's Consumer Duty applies to ALL regulated products including EPM:**

**Four Outcomes:**

| Outcome | Requirement | EPM Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| Products & Services | Products designed to meet target market needs | TMD required (see Section 4) |
| Price & Value | Fair value for consumer | Fees, margin, total cost must be justifiable |
| Consumer Understanding | Consumer understands product | Clear illustrations, plain English, adviser support |
| Consumer Support | Ongoing support throughout lifecycle | Customer dashboard, adviser access, complaints |

**Cross-cutting Rules:**
1. **Act in good faith** — No hidden terms, no small print surprises
2. **Avoid causing foreseeable harm** — If EPM is unsuitable, don't sell it
3. **Enable and support consumers** — Especially vulnerable consumers

### 3.3 Vulnerability Assessment

**FCA defines vulnerability as:**
> "A vulnerable consumer is someone who, due to their personal circumstances, is especially susceptible to harm, particularly when a firm is not acting with appropriate levels of care."

**Vulnerability Drivers (FCA FG21/1):**

| Driver | Examples | EPM Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Health | Physical disability, mental health, cognitive decline | High — target demographic is elderly |
| Life events | Bereavement, divorce, job loss, retirement | High — EPM often triggered by life event |
| Resilience | Low income, debt, low savings | Medium — EPM provides income |
| Capability | Low financial literacy, language barriers, digital exclusion | High — product is complex |

**EPM Must:**
- [ ] Train all advisers on vulnerability identification
- [ ] Provide alternative communication channels (phone, in-person, large print)
- [ ] Allow Power of Attorney representatives to act
- [ ] Flag vulnerability indicators in application process
- [ ] Escalate vulnerable consumers to senior adviser
- [ ] Document vulnerability assessment in suitability report

## 4. TARGET MARKET DETERMINATION (TMD)

### 4.1 Product Governance (PROD)

**Under FCA PROD rules (Product Intervention and Product Governance Sourcebook):**

Manufacturers (FutureProof/lender) must define:

**Target Market:**
- Age: 55+ (aligned with equity release)
- Property: Freehold or long leasehold (>70 years remaining)
- Property value: Minimum £150,000 (viable for income generation)
- Existing mortgage: None, or small balance that EPM can clear
- Income need: Supplementary retirement income
- Risk tolerance: Low (consumer bears no investment risk)
- Estate planning: Accepts reduced equity inheritance for immediate income

**Negative Target Market (NOT suitable for):**
- Consumers under 55 (regulatory restriction)
- Consumers who need to move in near future (EPM ties up property)
- Consumers with high existing mortgage (LVR would exceed 80%)
- Consumers who cannot maintain property (rates, insurance, repairs)
- Consumers who do not understand estate impact on beneficiaries
- Consumers with better alternatives available (e.g., sufficient pension, downsizing preferred)
- Consumers in financial distress (EPM is not a crisis product)

### 4.2 Distribution Strategy

| Channel | Requirements | EPM Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Direct (FutureProof platform) | Advised only (MCOB 12.3) | Online fact-find → phone/video adviser → application |
| Broker/intermediary | Must hold equity release permissions | Authorised broker network (ERC members) |
| IFA referral | Independent adviser recommends | Referral agreement with fee disclosure |

### 4.3 Ongoing Product Review

**PROD requires manufacturers to regularly review product:**
- Annual review of target market appropriateness
- Monitor complaint data for suitability issues
- Review distribution channels for mis-selling signals
- Report to board on product governance outcomes
- Update TMD if market conditions change

**Triggers for Immediate TMD Review:**
- Significant change in property market (>15% decline)
- Change in interest rates (>200bps movement)
- Regulatory change (FCA rule update)
- Material complaint trend (>5% of consumers)
- Investment portfolio underperformance (below pessimistic scenario)

## 7. INHERITANCE TAX (IHT) — MAJOR SELLING POINT

### 7.1 IHT Overview

**UK Inheritance Tax applies to estates above the nil-rate band:**

| Threshold | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nil-rate band (NRB) | £325,000 | Frozen until 2028 |
| Residence nil-rate band (RNRB) | £175,000 | Additional, if passing residence to direct descendants |
| Combined (individual) | £500,000 | NRB + RNRB |
| Combined (married couple) | £1,000,000 | Transferable between spouses |
| IHT rate above threshold | **40%** | One of the highest in the world |

### 7.2 How EPM Interacts with IHT

**This is EPM's strongest UK selling point.** The mechanics:

**Without EPM:**
| Asset | Value | IHT Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Home | £800,000 | Included in estate |
| Other assets | £200,000 | Included in estate |
| **Total estate** | **£1,000,000** | |
| Less: NRB + RNRB | -£500,000 | |
| **Taxable estate** | **£500,000** | |
| **IHT payable** | **£200,000** | 40% × £500,000 |

**With EPM (£640,000 loan at 80% LVR):**
| Asset | Value | IHT Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Home | £800,000 | Included in estate |
| Less: EPM mortgage | -£640,000 | **Liability reduces estate** |
| Net home equity | £160,000 | |
| Investment portfolio (grown) | £720,000+ | Included in estate |
| Other assets | £200,000 | Included in estate |
| **Total estate** | **£1,080,000** | Portfolio growth increases estate |
| Less: NRB + RNRB | -£500,000 | |
| **Taxable estate** | **£580,000** | |
| **IHT payable** | **£232,000** | 40% × £580,000 |

**Wait — IHT is HIGHER with EPM?**

Not necessarily. The key variables:

1. **Portfolio growth vs. mortgage balance:** If portfolio outperforms the mortgage interest (lender margin), net estate grows — but consumer received income during lifetime
2. **Income already consumed:** Monthly EPM income spent by consumer is NOT in the estate. Over 20 years at £2,500/month = £600,000 consumed
3. **Net benefit:** Consumer enjoyed £600,000 tax-free income during lifetime, while estate position may be similar or slightly worse

**The REAL IHT Selling Point:**

> "EPM converts illiquid home equity into **tax-free monthly income** during your lifetime. While the estate calculation is complex, the income you receive is NOT subject to IHT because it's been spent. Your beneficiaries may inherit less home equity but the investment portfolio provides ongoing value."

### 7.3 IHT Planning Integration

**EPM adviser must discuss:**

| Topic | Detail | Mandatory? |
|---|---|---|
| Current IHT position | Estimate estate value, NRB, RNRB | ✅ Yes |
| EPM impact on estate | Model estate with/without EPM | ✅ Yes |
| Beneficiary consultation | Encourage consumer to discuss with family | ⚠️ Recommended |
| IHT mitigation options | Gifting, trusts, pension contributions | ⚠️ General only (not tax advice) |
| Specialist referral | Recommend IHT specialist / solicitor | ✅ Yes |

**Disclaimer Required:**
> "FutureProof does not provide tax advice. The inheritance tax implications of an Equity Preservation Mortgage are complex and depend on your individual circumstances. We strongly recommend you consult a qualified tax adviser or solicitor before proceeding."

## 8. PSD2 / STRONG CUSTOMER AUTHENTICATION (SCA)

### 8.1 Overview

**Payment Services Directive 2 (PSD2)** applies to EPM if:
- FutureProof initiates payments (income disbursements to consumer)
- FutureProof accesses consumer bank accounts (open banking for verification)

### 8.2 SCA Requirements

**Strong Customer Authentication required for:**

| Action | SCA Required? | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Login to platform | ✅ Yes (if accessing financial data) | 2FA (SMS/app/biometric) |
| Viewing account balance | ⚠️ Depends on implementation | Session-based SCA |
| Changing bank details | ✅ Yes | Full SCA + confirmation period |
| Initiating withdrawal/payout | ✅ Yes | Full SCA |
| Signing documents | ✅ Yes | eIDAS-compliant e-signature |

**SCA = Two of Three Factors:**
1. **Knowledge** — Password, PIN
2. **Possession** — Phone, hardware token
3. **Inherence** — Fingerprint, facial recognition

### 8.3 Open Banking Integration

**If using open banking (Account Information Services):**
- FutureProof must be registered as AISP (Account Information Service Provider) with FCA
- OR use authorised AISP provider (TrueLayer, Plaid, Yapily)
- Consumer explicitly consents to data access
- Access refreshed every 90 days (re-consent)

**EPM Use Case:** Verify consumer income, bank balances, spending patterns for suitability assessment. Reduces manual document collection.

### 8.4 Platform Implementation

- [ ] 2FA implemented (SMS + authenticator app options)
- [ ] SCA enforced on login, bank detail changes, document signing
- [ ] Open banking integration (via TrueLayer/Plaid) for income verification
- [ ] 90-day re-consent flow for ongoing open banking access

## 9. FINANCIAL OMBUDSMAN SERVICE (FOS)

### 9.1 Overview

**FOS handles consumer complaints against FCA-authorised firms:**

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| Jurisdiction | All FCA-authorised firms (mandatory) |
| Complainant | Individual consumers, micro-enterprises, charities (<£6.5M) |
| Time limit | 6 years from event, or 3 years from awareness |
| Award limit | £415,000 (mortgage/equity release complaints) |
| Process | Internal → FOS referral → investigation → decision |
| Cost to firm | Case fee: £750 per complaint (first 3 free per year) |

### 9.2 Internal Dispute Resolution (IDR) — Mandatory First Step

**DISP rules (FCA Dispute Resolution sourcebook):**

| Requirement | Timeline | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Acknowledge complaint | Within 5 business days | Written acknowledgement |
| Investigate | Promptly | Thorough, fair investigation |
| Final response | Within 8 weeks | Written resolution or deadlock letter |
| Deadlock letter | At 8 weeks | If unresolved, inform consumer of FOS right |
| Record keeping | 3 years minimum | All complaints and outcomes |

### 9.3 EPM-Specific Complaint Risks

| Risk Area | Likely Complaint | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Income lower than projected | "I was told I'd get £3,200/month but only getting £2,100" | Clear disclaimers: projections not guarantees; pessimistic scenario shown |
| Suitability | "EPM was wrong for me, I should have downsized" | Robust suitability report; alternatives discussed and documented |
| Estate impact | "My children didn't know the house was mortgaged" | Beneficiary awareness prompt; documented in fact-find |
| Adviser conduct | "The adviser pressured me into signing" | Cooling-off period enforced; adviser compliance monitoring |
| Fees | "I didn't understand the fees were this high" | Transparent fee disclosure; total cost illustration |

- [ ] Deadlock letter template created

## 10. UK GDPR & DATA PROTECTION ACT 2018

### 10.1 Key Requirements

| Integrity & confidentiality | Appropriate security measures | Encryption, access controls, audit trails |

| Marketing communications | **Consent** | Art 6(1)(a) |
| Fraud prevention | Legal obligation (MLR) | Art 6(1)(c) |

### 10.3 Data Subject Rights

## 11. FINANCIAL CRIME — MONEY LAUNDERING REGULATIONS 2017

### 11.1 Overview

**The Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds Regulations 2017 (MLR 2017) apply to:**
- Credit institutions
- Financial institutions
- Estate agents (property transactions)

**FutureProof is caught as a credit/financial institution.**

### 11.2 Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

**Standard CDD (all consumers):**

| Check | Requirement | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Identity verification | Government-issued photo ID | Passport, driving licence |
| Address verification | Utility bill, bank statement (<3 months) | Digital upload or eIDV |
| Source of wealth | How did they acquire the property? | Declaration + supporting docs |
| Purpose of EPM | Why are they taking EPM? | Fact-find question |
| Beneficial ownership | Who benefits? | Consumer + named beneficiaries |

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) — triggered by:**
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) — consumer or close associate
- High-value property (>£2M)
- Complex ownership structure (trusts, corporate)
- High-risk country connection (FATF grey/black list)
- Unusual transaction pattern

**Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) — allowed when:**
- Low risk assessment
- UK-resident consumer with mainstream bank account
- Standard property value
- Clear source of wealth (employment/pension)

### 11.3 Ongoing Monitoring

| Obligation | Frequency | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Transaction monitoring | Ongoing | Flag unusual income disbursement patterns |

## 12. EQUITY RELEASE COUNCIL (ERC) MEMBERSHIP

### 12.1 Why Join the ERC?

**Not legally mandatory, but effectively essential for UK equity release:**

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| Consumer trust | ERC logo = trusted brand; consumers expect it |
| Adviser network | Access to ERC-member advisers (CeRER qualified) |
| Product standards | ERC standards exceed FCA minimum |
| No negative equity guarantee | ERC requires members to offer NNEG (EPM already does) |
| Right to remain | ERC standard (EPM already complies) |
| Industry data | Access to ERC market data and benchmarking |
| Broker distribution | Many brokers only recommend ERC-member products |

### 12.2 ERC Standards (Above FCA Minimum)

| Standard | ERC Requirement | EPM Compliance |
|---|---|---|
| NNEG | Mandatory for all products | ✅ Inherent in EPM design |
| Right to remain | Lifetime occupancy guaranteed | ✅ Built into contract |
| Fixed or capped rates | Interest must be fixed or capped | ⚠️ Check — lender margin may need to be fixed |
| Portability | Consumer can transfer to new property | ⚠️ Needs implementation — move home, take EPM with you |
| Independence | Adviser must be independent (whole of market) | ✅ Separate from lender |
| Transparency | Full cost disclosure | ✅ Personalised illustration covers this |
